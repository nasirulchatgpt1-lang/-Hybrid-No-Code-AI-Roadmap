Core Skills & Tool Stack

The primary platforms and languages you will master on this journey, sorted by importance.

Visual Development & Design

  • Webflow: Your primary tool for building polished, high-performance websites and landing pages for clients and your own products.
  • Bubble: Your go-to platform for developing complex, data-driven web applications, MVPs, and SaaS front-ends.
  • Figma: The essential first step for all projects, used for UI/UX design, wireframing, and creating a clear visual blueprint.

Automation & Integration

  • n8n: The central nervous system of your stack. Used for everything from simple workflows to complex AI agent orchestration and middleware.
  • Airtable: A flexible and powerful database used for project backends, CMS, and managing data for your automations.
  • Webhooks & APIs: The fundamental language of the internet, used constantly to connect all your tools and build custom integrations.

Programming & AI Fundamentals

  • Python: The core programming language for the entire second half of the roadmap. Essential for AI, scripting, and backend logic.
  • LangChain: The foundational framework for building LLM applications, RAG systems, and your first AI agents.
  • NLP Concepts (NLTK, spaCy): Critical skills for understanding and processing text data before feeding it to LLMs or ML models.
  • OpenAI / Anthropic APIs: Direct access to powerful LLMs like GPT-4 and Claude, the engines behind your AI applications.
  • Vector Databases (Pinecone, FAISS): The cornerstone of RAG and long-term memory for AI agents.

Advanced AI Engineering & Frameworks

  • CrewAI: Your primary framework for building collaborative, multi-agent teams to solve complex problems.
  • LangGraph: The go-to framework for building robust, stateful agents that require cyclical logic and memory.
  • AutoGen & OpenAI Agents SDK: Specialized frameworks for building code-executing agents and understanding core agent mechanics.
  • Voice AI Platforms (Retell, VAPI): A high-value specialization for building real-time, voice-interactive AI agents.
  • Model Context Protocol (MCP): An advanced concept for creating interoperable AI tools, placing you at the cutting edge of the field.

The Detailed 24-Week Roadmap

Follow this granular, hour-by-hour plan to build skills methodically. Each week is a 20-hour sprint (5 days, 4 hours/day).

Phase 1: No-Code Foundation (Weeks 1-8, 20 hrs/week)

Your goal is to become a proficient visual developer, capable of building and launching professional web assets.

Skills Learned: Figma, Webflow, Bubble, Airtable, Notion, Basic Automation
Week 1 — Design Fundamentals & Figma Mastery

Weekly Goal: Understand the principles of good web design and gain proficiency in Figma by designing a complete, multi-page website wireframe.

Tools/Skills: Figma, UI/UX Principles (Layout, Typography, Color).

Source Material: `Complete Web Design from Figma to Webflow` - Modules 02-05. ~2h 20m

Daily Breakdown (4 hours/day):

  • Day 1: Layout & Hierarchy.
    • Hour 1: Watch Module 03 videos on Alignment, Grid, and Hierarchy.
    • Hour 2-3: Recreate the examples from the videos in Figma. Practice aligning elements and establishing clear visual hierarchy on a blank canvas.
    • Hour 4: Start the Weekly Project: Create the wireframe for the homepage hero section.
  • Day 2: Typography.
    • Hour 1-2: Watch Module 04 videos on Typeface Personality, Categories, and Setting Type.
    • Hour 3: Complete the "Typeface Pairing Practice" exercise. Find and pair two fonts for the weekly project.
    • Hour 4: Apply your chosen typography to the homepage wireframe.
  • Day 3: Color & Imagery.
    • Hour 1-2: Watch Module 05 videos on Sampling, Fine-tuning Colors, and Brand Colors.
    • Hour 3: Use a color palette generator to create a brand palette. Practice applying it to simple shapes in Figma.
    • Hour 4: Add color and placeholder images to the homepage wireframe.
  • Day 4: Advanced Design & Other Pages.
    • Hour 1: Watch Module 06-07 videos on Image Overlays, Cropping, and Design Tricks.
    • Hour 2-3: Design the wireframes for the 'About' and 'Services' pages for the weekly project.
    • Hour 4: Create reusable components in Figma (buttons, navbars) for consistency.
  • Day 5: Finalizing the Design.
    • Hour 1-2: Design the wireframe for the 'Contact' page, including a form layout.
    • Hour 3: Review all four pages for consistency in spacing, typography, and color.
    • Hour 4: Organize your Figma file with clear naming and grouping. Prepare it for handoff to development (which you'll do next week).

Weekly Project: "Innovate AI" Startup Website Design

Goal: Design a complete 4-page website for a fictional AI consulting startup.

  1. Define Structure: Plan four pages: Homepage, About Us, Services, and Contact.
  2. Wireframe Layouts: In Figma, create low-fidelity wireframes for each page, focusing on element placement (nav, hero, feature sections, footer).
  3. Develop a Style Guide: Choose a primary and secondary font. Define a color palette (2-3 main colors, plus neutrals). Create component styles for headings, paragraphs, and buttons.
  4. Build High-Fidelity Mockups: Apply your style guide to the wireframes, adding placeholder text (Lorem Ipsum) and stock images to create a polished, realistic design.
  5. Prototype Basic Interactions: Connect the pages using Figma's prototyping tool so that clicking a navigation link takes you to the correct page.

    • Hour 1-2: Watch Module 14 videos on Responsive Web Design.

    • Hour 1: Build the Contact page and style the Webflow form.
    • Hour 2: Watch Module 15 videos on SEO settings. Apply meta titles and descriptions to all pages.

Weekly Project: Launch the "Innovate AI" Startup Website

Goal: Take the Figma design from last week and build it into a live, responsive website on Webflow.

  1. Project Setup: Create a blank project in Webflow. Set up your global font styles and color swatches to match your Figma style guide.
  2. Build Page by Page: Start with the Homepage. Use divs, containers, and sections to structure your page. Apply classes for styling, mirroring your Figma design pixel-for-pixel. Use Flexbox heavily for layout control.

  4. Optimize for Responsiveness: Use Webflow's built-in breakpoints (Tablet, Mobile Landscape, Mobile Portrait) to adjust your design. Ensure the site is perfectly usable and looks great on all screen sizes.
  5. Publish: Configure basic on-page SEO settings (title tags, meta descriptions) and publish the website to its staging domain. You now have a live URL to share in your portfolio.

Weekly Project: "UpVote" - A Reddit/Product Hunt Clone

Goal: Build a community platform where users can submit "posts" (e.g., ideas, links, products) and other users can upvote them. This project heavily relies on data relationships.

  1. Database Design: A `User` thing. A `Post` thing with fields like `Title`, `Content`, and `Creator` (type: User). Crucially, add a field on `Post` called `Upvoters` which is a **list of Users**. Also add an `Upvote Count` (type: number).
  2. Submitting Posts: Create a form for logged-in users to create a new `Post`.
  3. The Upvote Workflow: On the main repeating group of posts, add an "upvote" icon. The workflow for this icon will:
    • Make changes to Current cell's Post: `Upvoters` **add** `Current User`.
    • Make changes to Current cell's Post: `Upvote Count` = `This Post's Upvote Count + 1`.
    • Add a conditional to the icon: It should be unclickable or look different if `Current cell's Post's Upvoters` **contains** `Current User`.
  4. Sorting: On the main page, sort the repeating group of posts by `Upvote Count` in descending order, so the most popular posts are always at the top.

Monetization & Career Path

This isn't just a learning path; it's a structured journey to a high-income career. Here’s how you'll turn skills into revenue at each stage.

Phase 1: The No-Code Freelancer (Months 1-3)

Your Identity: A professional visual developer who can rapidly design, build, and launch beautiful, functional websites and basic apps.

Target Roles:

Webflow Developer, Figma Designer, No-Code Specialist, Junior Digital Consultant.

Primary Offerings (Services):

  • Figma to Webflow Conversion: Your bread and butter. A high-demand service for agencies and startups. ($1,500 - $3,000+)
  • CMS-Driven Business Websites: Offer dynamic sites like blogs, portfolios, or real estate listings. ($2,000 - $4,000+)
  • Basic Automation Setup: A great "foot-in-the-door" service. E.g., Webflow Form -> Airtable -> Email Notification. ($300 - $750)
  • Notion Client Portals: Offer this as a value-add or a standalone product for fellow freelancers. ($250 - $500)

Key Actions & Strategy:

Your goal is to get your first 3-5 paying clients. Focus on building a strong foundation.

  • Build a stunning portfolio website in Webflow showcasing your milestone projects.
  • Create 100% complete, highly-specialized profiles on Upwork and Fiverr.
  • "Productize" your services by creating fixed-price packages in your Upwork Project Catalog.
  • Actively send 5-10 high-quality, personalized proposals per week. Your goal is conversations, not just contracts.

Phase 2: The Automation & MVP Developer (Months 4-6)

Your Identity: A full-stack no-code developer and automation specialist who can build interactive web applications and solve complex business problems.

Target Roles:

Bubble Developer, Automation Consultant, No-Code/Low-Code Engineer, Technical Project Manager.

Primary Offerings (Services):

  • MVP Development in Bubble: Build the first version of a SaaS product for a startup founder. ($5,000 - $15,000+)
  • Custom Business Automation Systems: Create intelligent systems like the "Support Ticket Router" milestone. ($3,000 - $7,000+)
  • API Integration & Middleware: Connect disparate business systems for clients using n8n. ($1,000 - $5,000)
  • No-Code Backend Development: Build complex logic and databases in Bubble or Airtable/n8n for other developers. ($75 - $150/hr)

Key Actions & Strategy:

Shift from freelance platforms to building a direct client base and reputation.

  • Update your portfolio with detailed case studies of your app and automation projects. Emphasize business results.
  • Build a professional presence on LinkedIn. Share your work and connect with startup founders and technical recruiters.
  • Niche down. Become the "go-to" automation expert for a specific industry (e.g., e-commerce, real estate, finance).
  • Start transitioning from fixed-price projects to hourly or weekly retainer contracts for ongoing development and support.

Phase 3: The AI Engineer (Months 7-12)

Your Identity: A hybrid AI Engineer who architects and builds intelligent systems, RAG applications, and autonomous agents.

Target Roles:

AI Engineer, Agentic AI Developer, Automation Architect, AI Consultant, Prompt Engineer (Senior).

Primary Offerings (Services):

  • Custom AI Agent Development: Build autonomous agents like the "NexusLeads" project for businesses. ($15,000 - $30,000+)
  • RAG & Knowledge Base Chatbots: Create internal or customer-facing chatbots that use company data. ($5,000 - $10,000)
  • AI Voice Agent Development: A highly specialized and valuable service for customer support or sales. ($10,000+)
  • Full-Time Employment: You are now a strong candidate for full-time, remote-friendly AI Engineer roles. ($100,000 - $180,000+ / year)

Key Actions & Strategy:

Establish yourself as an expert in the applied AI space.

  • Create video demos of your AI agents in action; showing is more powerful than telling.
  • Contribute to AI discussions on platforms like LinkedIn and X (Twitter) to build a professional brand.
  • Start applying for selective full-time roles that match your hybrid skillset. Emphasize your ability to bridge no-code front-ends with Python backends.
  • Begin pitching high-ticket projects directly to businesses, focusing on the ROI your custom agents can provide.

Phase 4: The AI Architect & Founder (Year 2+)

Your Identity: A leader and product visionary who designs complex AI solutions, builds products, and drives business value at the highest level.

Target Roles:

AI Solution Architect, Technical Co-Founder, SaaS Founder, Principal AI Engineer.

Primary Offerings (Services):

  • Launch a Micro-SaaS Product: Productize your final capstone project and build a recurring revenue stream. (Uncapped Potential)
  • High-Ticket AI Strategy Consulting: Advise larger companies on how to implement agentic AI workflows. ($250 - $500+/hour, $25k+ projects)
  • Build and Lead a Team: As an agency owner or a lead engineer, you will design systems and manage other developers.
  • Angel Investing / Advising: Use your deep technical and product knowledge to help early-stage AI startups.

Key Actions & Strategy:

Transition from "doing the work" to "designing the systems" and building assets.

  • Focus on marketing your SaaS product on platforms like Product Hunt and Indie Hackers.
  • Build a personal brand by writing about your journey and sharing your expertise in AI architecture.
  • Network with venture capitalists and other founders in the AI space.
  • Continuously learn and experiment with the absolute latest advancements in AI to stay at the cutting edge.

Scaling Your Career

Move from freelancer to business owner and expert.

Business Scaling

  • Productize Your Services: Instead of custom quotes, sell fixed-price packages (e.g., "AI Chatbot Setup: $2,000"). This simplifies sales and delivery.
  • Build a Niche Reputation: Become the go-to expert for a specific industry (e.g., "AI automation for law firms"). This allows you to charge premium rates.
  • Create Retainer Offers: Sell ongoing "Automation Maintenance" or "AI Strategy" retainers for predictable, recurring monthly revenue ($500 - $5,000/mo).
  • Build & Sell Templates: Create high-quality Webflow, Bubble, or n8n templates and sell them on marketplaces for passive income.

Technical Scaling

  • Master One Stack Deeply: While this roadmap is broad, true expertise comes from deep knowledge. Choose to specialize in either the Bubble or advanced AI agent stack.
  • Contribute to Open Source: If you become proficient in Python, contributing to libraries like LangChain can rapidly build your reputation.
  • Build a Micro-SaaS: Apply all your skills to solve a single, painful problem. Build an MVP in Bubble, automate it with n8n and AI, and launch it. This is the ultimate path to uncapped income and freedom.
  • Specialize in a High-Demand AI Field: Focus on areas like AI-driven security, ethical AI consulting, or building specialized financial models.
